Unit 14

Past Perfect (I had done)

Rule
The past perfect simple is formed using had + past participle (like gone, seen, or finished).
Concept
We use it to talk about things that happened before a starting point in the past.
Example
"When Sarah arrived at the party, Nick had already gone home."

Unit 14

Connecting to the Present vs. the Past

Present Perfect
Connects a past action to now.

"We aren't hungry. We've just had lunch."
Past Perfect
Connects a past action to an earlier past.

"We weren't hungry. We'd just had lunch."

Unit 14

Sequence of Events

Simple Past
Events happening in chronological order.

"Was Tom there when you arrived? Yes, but he left soon afterwards."
Past Perfect
An event already completed when another started.

"Was Tom there when you arrived? No, he'd already left."

Unit 15

Past Perfect Continuous (I had been doing)

Rule
Formed using had been + -ing (like doing, working, or playing).
Concept
You can say that something had been happening before something else happened.
Example
"The sun was shining, but the ground was very wet. It had been raining."

Unit 15

Before Now vs. Before Then

Present Perfect Continuous
Action happening before now.

"I hope the bus comes soon. I've been waiting for 20 minutes."
Past Perfect Continuous
Action happening before a time in the past.

"The bus finally came. I'd been waiting for 20 minutes."

Unit 15

Non-Continuous Verbs

Rule
Some verbs are not normally used in continuous forms.
Correct Example
"We had known each other for years."
⚠️ Do not say: "had been knowing" — stative verbs like know, have, and like take the simple form, not the continuous.
